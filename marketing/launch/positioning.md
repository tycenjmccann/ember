# Ember — Positioning

## Positioning statement

For developers and teams who want agentic coding without handing their code to a vendor's
cloud, **Ember** is an open-source coding agent — Claude Code + Codex — that runs inside your
*own* AWS account and keeps your session warm across every device. Fire a task at your desk,
close the laptop, and the work keeps running on a warm micro-VM in your cloud; reopen on the
web, your phone, or back home and it's exactly where you left it. Unlike Claude Code on the
web or OpenAI Codex/Ona — which lock the agent to *their* cloud and *their* model — with Ember
the code, the credentials, the audit logs, and the model choice all stay in your account. Same
capability; your ground.

---

## Taglines (lead line first)

1. **Keep your session warm.** *(primary)*
2. Close the lid. It's still burning.
3. Your fire. Your land.
4. The build keeps burning while you don't.
5. Go outside. It's handled.

---

## Elevator pitch (~30 sec)

You know how Claude Code and Codex now run "on the web" — fire off a task, close your laptop,
pick it back up later? Ember does that, but it runs entirely inside your *own* AWS account
instead of a vendor's cloud. One command stands up a per-session micro-VM and a web UI; you
connect the Claude or ChatGPT plan you already pay for, so there's zero marginal LLM cost — or
use Bedrock pay-per-token in your VPC if compliance needs it. Close the laptop and the session
stays warm; reopen on any device and it's still building. Your code, your keys, your bill, your
model — nothing ever leaves your account. It's open-core: MIT to self-host, with a paid
enterprise tier for SSO, VPC isolation, and audit. The incumbents validated agentic coding and
then locked it to their cloud. Ember keeps it on your ground — so you can step away from the
terminal and actually go outside while the loop runs.
